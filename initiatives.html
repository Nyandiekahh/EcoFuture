Featured Initiative: Coastal Restoration Project

Our flagship coastal restoration project focuses on rehabilitating damaged shoreline ecosystems along BC's coast. This multi-year initiative combines:

  • Regular cleanup operations to remove plastic pollution
  • Native plant restoration to prevent erosion
  • Water quality monitoring and improvement
  • Community education about marine conservation

Since 2022, we've restored over 5 kilometers of coastline, with measurable improvements in biodiversity and ecosystem health.

Top 10 Eco-Friendly Living Tips

Simple changes you can make today to reduce your environmental footprint:

  1. Reduce Single-Use Plastics

    Use reusable bags, bottles, and containers whenever possible.

  2. Conserve Water

    Fix leaks promptly and install water-efficient fixtures.

  3. Choose Sustainable Transportation

    Walk, cycle, or use public transit when possible.

  4. Eat Local and Seasonal

    Support local farmers and reduce food transportation emissions.

  5. Reduce Energy Consumption

    Turn off lights and unplug electronics when not in use.

  6. Compost Food Waste

    Keep organic material out of landfills where it produces methane.

  7. Choose Eco-Friendly Products

    Look for certified sustainable and non-toxic options.

  8. Plant Native Species

    Support local biodiversity in your garden.

  9. Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

    In that order! Reducing consumption has the biggest impact.

  10. Advocate for Change

    Use your voice and vote to support environmental policies.
